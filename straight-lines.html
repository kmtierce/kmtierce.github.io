straight lines (1978 – 1980)

This group began with two remaining members (Emitt & Thomas) from Pressure Point. Pressure Point formed in Biloxi MS in 1978 and moved to New England to begin touring in October 1978. Pressure Point was terminated when the drummer was severely injured in an auto accident in late Spring of 1978. Emitt and Thomas stayed together with a trio formed with a drummer from Rhode Island during the Summer of 1979. The Verbs (Emitt on bass/vocals, Thomas on guitar/vocals, Steven Rabbit on drums) rehearsed cover tunes but never performed publicly.

During rehearsals with The Verbs, Emitt renewed a friendship with a former acquaintance who was looking for a group to fund and represent. This new representative helped Emitt and Thomas set up a recording session for a new group that would become Straight Lines. Thomas knew a bass player (Tim Thrasher) and brought him aboard for the recording session. The drummer from The Verbs was jettisoned from the group and a friend and drummer (Derek Blevins) from another Boston area group (Johanna Wild) was hired for the recording session that took place in 3 studios from the Fall of 1979 to early 1980. The result was 5 original songs written by Emitt and one co-written with Thomas.

With the recording in hand Emitt, Thomas and Tim began the search for a drummer and the final piece that would become Straight Lines. Enter Larry Jaworske, former drummer for Arion Deuce which originated in Cleveland, Ohio, toured to New England and then disbanded. With Larry on board Straight Lines was complete and the band began rehearsals in a rented house in Hull MA.
