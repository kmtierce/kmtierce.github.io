bad habits (1980 – 1982)

After the breakup of Straight Lines in late 1980, Emitt and Rick look for the next group. Rick had played with Canadian bass player Ivan Boudreau now living in New Hampshire. Emitt, Rick and Ivan set up a rehearsal space in downtown Lawrence MA above a biker club (story for another day) and begin auditioning drummers.

A music graduate in Greg Conroy fills the spot. Emitt writes new songs for the group and their representative (from Straight Lines) keeps the group booked constantly allowing them to have a well polished live show. This lineup does one live recording session at Studio B in Boston just up the street from the studio owned by The Cars. The group continued into 1981 until a music and material impass between Emitt and Ivan ended with Ivan leaving the group.

Emitt, Rick and Greg relocate rehearsal to downtown Boston sharing space with Mission of Burma and begin auditioning for a bass player. Roommates Dan Nuzzi (keyboards) and John McArdle (bass) are added and a new sound is born for Bad Habits.
